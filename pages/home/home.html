The NeuroXR Lab embraces advanced visual arts technology to develop innovative ways to assess and improve mental health through both research and development. Led by personnel from the IUPUI School of Informatics and the IU School of Medicine Department of Psychiatry, we conduct research aimed at increasing the options for clinicians and patients to address mental health concerns through advanced technology such as virtual reality or augmented reality. Our team and facilities are also designed to be a resource for investigators in Indiana and beyond who aim to develop new technology to improve physical or mental health as part of training, teaching, assessment, or therapy.

RESEARCH

We investigate how novel virtual reality programs can shape thoughts and behavior, using VR to give users embodied experiences that are otherwise impossible to provide. With targeted interventions and assessments, our research focuses on disruptive behavior disorders, ADHD, substance use disorders, and alcohol use disorders, from childhood to adulthood.

WHO WE ARE

The Indy NeuroXR Lab brings together faculty with psychiatric and informatics expertise to optimize XR research and development, as well as teams of students with cutting-edge knowledge. We have experience building novel XR programs from scratch and investigating how these programs impact the brain to improve mental health.
